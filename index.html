About Megan


I grew up in Ho-Ho-Kus, NJ, the only town in North America with two hyphens! Growing up I always wanted a sister, but instead my parents, Julie and Michael, gave me four wonderful brothers (Chris, 24; Jonathan, 17; Teddy, 15; and Nicholas, 10). For high school I attended Villa Walsh Academy, an all girls Catholic school in Morristown. I then escaped winter and went to Stanford for college, where I majored in Classical Languages (Latin and Greek). I am currently earning my Master of Divinity at Notre Dame, where I am relearning how to deal with the cold.

About Michael


I was born in Fremont, CA to David and Marie Crayne, who had wanted a daughter but were just as joyful to be blessed with a son to be a younger brother to their eldest, Adam. I grew up in Union City, CA surrounded by my extended family (my mom is one of twelve and my dad is one of seven, so aunts, uncles, and cousins are never hard to find), which I loved, in no small part thanks to the numerous parties and endless supplies of food. I played trumpet throughout middle school and high school and met most of my friends through marching band, wind orchestra, and jazz band. I attended Stanford University where I put down the trumpet and picked up a compound bow, competing as a member of the Stanford Archery Team. I majored in Civil Engineering and minored in Italian, studying in beautiful Florence, Italy for a quarter. I work in the energy efficiency industry, which brought me to live in Portland, OR for a year and a half before I moved to South Bend, IN to be with Megan as she studies at Notre Dame.

Our Love Story

Although they had been on the archery team together for a year, it wasn’t until summer camp 2011 that Megan and Michael started really getting to know each other. It was around that time that he started toying with the idea of her being more than a friend, but this hadn’t yet crossed her mind. Michael put the thought aside for the summer and bided his time until the next school year.

When school started up again, Megan and Michael frequently practiced on the same days. As they spent more time together, Michael started realizing more and more that he wanted to be in a relationship with Megan. As November approached, he saw the perfect opportunity to finally asked her out. He gathered his courage, consulted with his roommate, and decided this was the moment. He headed to the range, his heart beating fast, and when he got there found Megan once again trying to convince him to grow out his beard for No Shave November. This time, Michael had his answer all planned out: “I’ll make a deal with you— I won’t shave for all of November if you’ll go on a date with me.” Somewhat caught off guard, Megan agreed to go out to froyo with Michael.

Neither of them knew what to expect, but they were both pleased to find that while they sat outside Yogurtland the hours melted away as they were drawn deeper into conversation. Since that night their hesitant feelings have grown into a deep love, and they can’t wait to spend the rest of their lives together!
